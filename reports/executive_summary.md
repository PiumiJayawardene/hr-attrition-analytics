# HR Attrition Analytics — Executive Summary

**Prepared by:** Piumi Jayawardene  
**Date:** 2026  
**Dataset:** IBM HR Analytics (1,470 employees)

---

## Situation
The company is experiencing a 16.1% annual attrition rate, 
significantly above the industry benchmark of 10–12%. 
This analysis identifies the primary drivers.

## Key Findings

| Finding | Detail | Impact |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Overtime is a major driver | 30.5% attrition among OT workers vs 10.4% | High |
| Sales dept most affected | 20.6% attrition rate | High |
| Salary gap | Leavers earn ~$2K/month less | Medium |
| Sales Reps at highest risk | 39.8% attrition rate | Critical |
| Low satisfaction predicts exit | Score 1 = 22% attrition | Medium |

## Recommendations
1. Audit overtime policies — Sales team is over-stretched
2. Conduct a salary benchmarking exercise for Sales Representatives
3. Launch quarterly pulse surveys targeting satisfaction score tracking
4. Implement a retention bonus programme for high-risk roles

## Estimated Impact
Reducing attrition by 5% retains ~74 employees/year. 
At avg replacement cost of 50% annual salary, this saves approximately
$1.3M per year in recruitment and onboarding costs.
